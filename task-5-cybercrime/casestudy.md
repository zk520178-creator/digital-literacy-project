# Phishing Case Study

Phishing is a type of cybercrime where attackers try to trick individuals into revealing sensitive information such as passwords, bank details, or OTPs. This is usually done through fake emails, messages, or websites that appear to be from trusted sources.

In a typical phishing attack, the victim receives an email or SMS that looks official, such as from a bank or online service. The message often creates urgency, asking the user to click a link and update their details. Once the user clicks the link, they are redirected to a fake website that looks identical to the real one. When the user enters their information, it is captured by the attacker.

Students and online users are common targets because they may not always verify links carefully. The consequences can include financial loss, identity theft, and unauthorized access to accounts.

Phishing attacks are increasing rapidly, making it important for users to stay alert and cautious while using the internet.
